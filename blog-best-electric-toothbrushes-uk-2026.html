🛒 Buying Guide

Oscillating vs sonic — which is better?

Both work. Oral-B's oscillating-rotating technology has more clinical research behind it. Philips Sonicare's sonic vibration is generally considered gentler and is often preferred by people with sensitive gums. There's no clear winner — it comes down to personal preference.

Do you need a pressure sensor?

Yes, if you tend to brush hard. Pressing too firmly with an electric toothbrush can damage enamel and gums over time. Most mid-range brushes (£40+) include a pressure sensor that alerts you when you're overbrushing. Worth the upgrade if you're a heavy brusher.

Replacement head costs — factor this in

Electric toothbrush heads need replacing every 3 months. Oral-B compatible heads are widely available from third parties at £1–3 each. Philips Sonicare heads tend to cost more. The brush itself is only part of the long-term cost.
