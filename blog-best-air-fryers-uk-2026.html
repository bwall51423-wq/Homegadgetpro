🛒 Air Fryer Buying Guide — What to Look For

Capacity — the most important decision

Get this wrong and you'll regret it. As a rough guide:

  • 1–2 people: 3–4L is fine. The Ninja PRO 4.7L is ideal.
  • Family of 3–4: You want at least 6–8L. The Russell Hobbs XXL or Ninja Dual Zone.
  • 5+ people or meal preppers: Go for 9L+ or a dual zone model.

Single drawer vs dual zone

Single drawer models are simpler, cheaper and perfectly capable. Dual zone models cost more but let you cook two different foods simultaneously at different temperatures — genuinely useful for families putting together a full meal.

⚠️
Don't underestimate how much space an air fryer takes up. Even a compact 4.7L model needs clearance above and around it. A 9L dual zone model is roughly the size of a microwave. Measure your worktop before buying.

Wattage

Most air fryers run between 1,400W and 2,400W. Higher wattage generally means faster preheating and cooking. Don't worry too much about this — all mainstream models are efficient enough that the difference in energy cost is negligible.

Ease of cleaning

Check whether the basket and drawer are dishwasher safe. Most Ninja models are. If you're buying a budget model, check reviews specifically for mentions of the non-stick coating deteriorating over time.
