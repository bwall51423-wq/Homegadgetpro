🛒 Electric Heater Buying Guide

Fan heater vs convector heater vs ceramic heater

Fan heaters use a heating element and a fan to blow warm air — heats a room quickly but noisy. Best for fast, short bursts of heat. Convector heaters use natural air circulation — quieter and better for sustained heating over longer periods. Ceramic heaters use a ceramic element that's more energy-efficient and safer to the touch — the premium choice for regular daily use.

Do you need a thermostat?

A thermostat is one of the most valuable features on any electric heater. Without one, a heater runs at full power until you manually switch it off. With a thermostat, the heater maintains your target temperature and cycles on and off as needed — using significantly less electricity over time. If you're using a heater regularly, a thermostat pays for itself in saved running costs.

⚠️
Running costs matter more than purchase price. A 2000W heater running for 4 hours a day costs roughly £1 per day at current UK electricity rates. Over a winter (say 120 days) that's £120 in electricity — far more than the heater itself. A thermostat that reduces average output by even 30% saves meaningful money.

Safety features to look for

Overheat protection is essential — it automatically cuts power if the heater gets too hot. Most modern heaters include this. Tip-over protection (the heater switches off if knocked over) is particularly important in homes with children or pets. Both are standard on reputable brands.
