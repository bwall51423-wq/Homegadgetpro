💇 Hair straightener buying guide

What actually makes a difference for your hair:

  • Plate material: Ceramic plates are the standard and suit most hair types. Titanium plates heat faster and stay hotter — better for very thick, coarse hair. Avoid cheap non-coated metal plates which snag and damage.
  • Temperature control: Fine or damaged hair: 160–180°C. Normal hair: 180–200°C. Thick or coarse hair: 200–230°C. Models with a fixed temperature (like ghd at 185°C) have done the research on the optimal setting — not a limitation, a feature.
  • Intelligent heat sensors: Monitors and corrects temperature spikes in real time. This is the key feature that prevents heat damage on coloured, bleached, or fine hair. Available from the Remington S8598 upwards.
  • Steam vs dry: Steam straighteners (L'Oréal SteamPod) infuse moisture during styling — resulting in less heat damage and longer-lasting straightness. Transformative for curly, frizzy, or coarse hair.
  • Plate width: Standard (25mm) plates suit most hair. Wide plates (38mm+) are significantly faster for thick, long, or very curly hair. Narrow plates give more control for short hair or bangs.
