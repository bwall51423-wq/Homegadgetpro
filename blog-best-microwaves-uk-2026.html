📡 Microwave buying guide

Here are the specs that actually change what you can cook:

  • Wattage: 900W heats more evenly and faster than 800W. For everyday reheating it's noticeable. Inverter models (Panasonic) deliver continuous 900W rather than pulsing on/off — the biggest heating quality upgrade available.
  • Capacity: 20L suits 1–3 people. 25L fits a family-sized bowl or covered plate. 27L fits a whole chicken. Most people underestimate how important this is until they try to fit a large dish.
  • Solo vs combination: A solo microwave reheats and defrosts. A combination model also grills and bakes — meaning it can genuinely replace a second oven in a compact kitchen.
  • Auto-cook programs: Presets for pizza, potato, fish, and vegetables are genuinely useful — press one button and walk away. Budget models often lack these.
  • Flatbed vs turntable: Flatbed models (Panasonic) have no carousel to wash and more usable interior space. Most affordable models still use turntables.
