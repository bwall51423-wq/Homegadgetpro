🛒 Headphone Buying Guide

Over-ear vs on-ear

Over-ear headphones cup your entire ear — generally more comfortable for long sessions and better at passive noise isolation. On-ear headphones sit on the ear — lighter and more portable but can cause discomfort over time. If you're planning to wear them for hours at a stretch, over-ear is worth the extra bulk.

Do you actually need ANC?

Active noise cancellation is genuinely useful on commutes, in open-plan offices or on flights. At home or in quiet environments it makes less difference. Budget ANC models (like the Soundcore Q20i) handle traffic and office noise well but won't match premium models on aircraft engine noise. If you mostly listen at home, save the money and get better audio quality without ANC instead.

⚠️
Watch out for unknown brands with very low review counts. The headphone category on Amazon is full of unbranded products with inflated ratings and very few genuine reviews. We've prioritised models with at least several hundred verified reviews — ideally thousands — to give you confidence in the data.

Battery life

Most wireless headphones now last 30–60 hours between charges. Unless you're a very heavy user, anything over 30 hours is more than adequate for a week of daily use. More useful to check is how long a quick charge takes — many modern headphones offer 10 minutes charging for several hours of playback.
