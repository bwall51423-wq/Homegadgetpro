🫖 Kettle buying guide

Here's what actually makes a difference in day-to-day use:

  • Wattage: 3kW boils 1.7L in under 3 minutes. Cheaper kettles may be 2–2.5kW — noticeably slower.
  • Capacity: 1.7L is the UK standard. If you mainly make 1–2 cups, a smaller 1L travel kettle saves energy.
  • Variable temperature: Green tea brews best at 80°C, white tea at 70°C. Budget kettles don't have this — if you're a serious tea drinker, it matters.
  • Keep warm: Holds temperature for 20–30 minutes. Useful when making multiple rounds. Usually only on mid-range and above.
  • Noise: Some kettles rattle loudly. If you have an open-plan kitchen or light sleepers nearby, check reviews specifically for noise comments.
