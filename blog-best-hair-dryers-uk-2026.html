🛒 Hair Dryer Buying Guide

How much wattage do you actually need?

1600–1800W is adequate for fine or short hair and occasional use. 2000–2200W suits most hair types for daily use — the most common range for household dryers. 2300W+ is for thick, long or curly hair where drying time is a real issue. Higher wattage means faster drying but also more weight and heat, so balance it against your hair type.

Ionic technology — is it worth it?

Ionic dryers emit negative ions that break down water molecules faster and help close the hair cuticle as it dries, reducing frizz and adding shine. It's genuinely effective, particularly for people with frizzy or thick hair. Most mid-range and above dryers include it. If frizz control matters to you, look for ionic technology in the product description.

⚠️
Heat damage is the biggest risk with cheap or careless dryer use. Always use the cool shot button at the end of drying — it closes the hair cuticle and seals in shine. Keep the dryer moving rather than focusing heat on one section, and keep it at least 15cm from your hair. A dryer with a heat protection mode (like Remington's OPTIheat) adds an extra safety layer.

Weight matters more than you think

If you're blow drying for 15 minutes every morning, a heavy dryer gets tiring quickly — particularly for longer styles. Budget dryers tend to be lighter because they have less powerful motors. If you have long hair and need a high-powered model, check the weight specification before buying — anything over 700g will feel heavy over a long drying session.
