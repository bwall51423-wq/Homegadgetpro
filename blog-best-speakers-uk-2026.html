🔊 Bluetooth speaker buying guide

  • Wattage (W): 5–12W suits indoor and quiet outdoor use. 20–30W fills a garden or patio. More watts means louder but also heavier and more expensive.
  • Battery life: 10 hours covers most home use. 20–24 hours suits full-day outdoor trips. Check whether the rating is at 50% or max volume — it makes a big difference.
  • Waterproofing: IPX5 handles splashes and rain. IPX7 means it can be submerged — essential for poolside, beach, or shower use.
  • Stereo vs mono: Most single-unit portables produce mono or simulated stereo. True stereo requires two speakers or a stereo-paired unit.
  • Bluetooth range: Most modern speakers reach 10m easily. Walls and obstacles reduce range. 5.0 or higher Bluetooth versions give better stability.
  • Aux input: Useful if you want to connect devices without Bluetooth or use it as a wired speaker. Less common on newer models.
