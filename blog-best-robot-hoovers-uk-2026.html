🛒 Robot Hoover Buying Guide

Navigation — the biggest difference between models

Random navigation (budget models) — the robot bounces around until it's covered the floor. It works, but it's inefficient and may miss patches. Lidar/laser mapping (premium models) — the robot scans the room and creates a precise map, cleaning in straight methodical lines. The difference in cleaning thoroughness is noticeable, especially in larger homes.

⚠️
Prepare your home before you buy. Robot hoovers work best in clear, uncluttered spaces. Loose cables, socks on the floor or fringe rugs will cause problems. Most robots also struggle with dark-coloured floors — they can confuse them with drop-offs. Check your specific model's reviews for floor type feedback.

Vacuum only vs vacuum and mop

If you have mostly hard floors — wood, tile or laminate — a 2-in-1 vacuum and mop model is worth considering. The mopping function won't replace a proper mop for deep cleaning, but it will keep hard floors looking clean day to day. For mainly carpeted homes, a vacuum-only model is fine and often has stronger suction.

Self-emptying stations — worth it?

Yes, if you can stretch to it. The convenience of not emptying the dustbin after every clean is genuinely appreciated once you have it. The eufy C10 at £169 is the most accessible self-empty option with a proven brand behind it.

Suction power — what do the numbers mean?

Pa (Pascals) measures suction strength. For hard floors and low-pile carpets, 3,000–4,000Pa is more than sufficient. For thick or high-pile carpets, look for 5,000Pa+. Beyond 7,000Pa you're in diminishing returns territory for most homes.
