💧 Dehumidifier buying guide

The UK's climate makes dehumidifiers one of the most useful home appliances you can own:

  • Compressor vs desiccant: Compressor models (most common) are more energy-efficient and extract more litres per day — but slow down significantly in cold rooms below 15°C. Desiccant models use a rotor instead of refrigeration and maintain extraction in cold garages, conservatories, and unheated spaces year-round.
  • Extraction rate (L/day): 10L/day suits average UK homes with mild condensation. 12L covers moderate damp and families. 20L handles serious damp, large properties, or whole-house use.
  • Noise: Dehumidifiers run for hours at a time. Quiet models (Meaco, typically under 40dB) can run in bedrooms. Louder models are better in utility rooms or kitchens.
  • Auto humidity sensor: Runs the unit only when humidity exceeds your set level. This saves energy and reduces noise vs running continuously.
  • Laundry mode: Runs at maximum extraction to dry clothes faster — a key UK feature given our wet climate. Most mid-range and above models have this.
