🛒 Smart Bulb Buying Guide

B22 bayonet vs E27 screw — which do you have?

UK homes traditionally use the B22 bayonet fitting — the push-and-twist style. E27 is the screw-in European style, now common in modern lamps and light fittings. Check your existing bulb before buying — the fitting type is printed on the base of most bulbs. Both are widely available in smart bulb form.

Do you need colour-changing?

Colour-changing bulbs are fun but for most rooms you'll use warm white 95% of the time. If you're kitting out a living room or bedroom lamp and want scheduling and dimming, the white-only Tapo L510B is £6 cheaper per 2-pack than the colour version and the light quality is often better for everyday use. Get colour for accent lights, entertainment rooms or kids' bedrooms.

⚠️
Important: smart bulbs need a 2.4GHz WiFi network. Most home routers broadcast both 2.4GHz and 5GHz — smart bulbs only work on 2.4GHz. If you have a mesh network or a router that combines both frequencies under one name, you may need to temporarily separate them during setup. This trips up a lot of buyers.

Do you need a hub?

The Tapo, Govee and Lepro bulbs in this roundup are all hub-free — they connect directly to your WiFi. You don't need to buy any additional hardware. Just install the bulb, download the app and you're up and running in minutes.
